What it does: Multiplies any two numbers using vertical and cross multiplication

Best for: 2-digit √ó 2-digit multiplication

Example: 23 √ó 14

What it does: Multiplies numbers close to base (10, 100, 1000) incredibly fast

Best for: Numbers near 10, 100, etc.

Example: 98 √ó 97 (both near 100)

Step 1: Find difference from base 100 98 ‚Üí -2 (2 less than 100) 97 ‚Üí -3 (3 less than 100) Step 2: Cross-subtract or cross-add 98 - 3 = 95 OR 97 - 2 = 95 (This gives first part) Step 3: Multiply differences (-2) √ó (-3) = 6 (This gives second part) Since base is 100, add 0 to make it 06 Answer: 9506 ‚úì

Example: 103 √ó 104 (both above 100)

103 ‚Üí +3 104 ‚Üí +4 Cross-add: 103 + 4 = 107 OR 104 + 3 = 107 Multiply: 3 √ó 4 = 12 Answer: 10712 ‚úì
