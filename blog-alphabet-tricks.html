Alphabet reasoning questions appear in every government exam - SSC CGL, Banking PO, Railway RRB, and State PSCs. While they seem simple, solving them quickly requires smart shortcuts. Here are 10 powerful tricks that toppers use to solve alphabet questions in seconds.

üéØ Trick #1: The EJOTY Method (Position Finding)

What it does: Finds any letter's position in under 2 seconds

How it works: Memorize E=5, J=10, O=15, T=20, Y=25. Then count forward/backward from nearest reference.

Question: What is the position of 'R'?
Solution: R is near T (20). T is 20, R comes 2 before ‚Üí 20-2 = 18
Answer: 18th position ‚úì

When to use: Direct position questions, alphabet test, coding-decoding

üîÑ Trick #2: Quick Opposite Letters (27-N Formula)

What it does: Finds opposite letter instantly without counting backwards

Formula: Opposite position = 27 - Original position

Question: What is the opposite of 'F'?
Solution: F = 6th position ‚Üí 27 - 6 = 21 ‚Üí U
Answer: U ‚úì

Quick opposites to memorize:

  • A ‚Üî Z
  • B ‚Üî Y
  • E ‚Üî V
  • J ‚Üî Q
  • O ‚Üî L

üìä Trick #3: Alphabet Series Pattern Recognition

What it does: Identifies patterns in letter series quickly

Common patterns:

Pattern 1: Skip Letters

Series: A, C, E, G, ?
Pattern: +2 (skipping 1 letter)
Answer: I ‚úì

Pattern 2: Alternate Forward-Backward

Series: A, Z, C, X, E, ?
Pattern: +2 from start, -2 from end alternately
Answer: V ‚úì

Pattern 3: Position-Based

Series: B(2), D(4), F(6), H(8), ?
Pattern: Letters at even positions
Answer: J(10) ‚úì

üßÆ Trick #4: Letter + Number Operations

What it does: Solves questions mixing letters with numbers

Method: Convert letter to position ‚Üí Do math ‚Üí Convert back to letter

Question: D + 5 = ?
Solution: D = 4, 4 + 5 = 9, 9 = I
Answer: I ‚úì
Question: P - 3 = ?
Solution: P = 16, 16 - 3 = 13, 13 = M
Answer: M ‚úì

üîÄ Trick #5: Letter Shifting (Caesar Cipher)

What it does: Decodes shifted alphabets in coding questions

Method: Find shift amount, apply to all letters

Question: If CAT is coded as FDW, what is DOG?
Solution:
C(3) ‚Üí F(6) = +3 shift
A(1) ‚Üí D(4) = +3 shift
T(20) ‚Üí W(23) = +3 shift
Apply to DOG:
D(4) + 3 = G(7)
O(15) + 3 = R(18)
G(7) + 3 = J(10)
Answer: GRJ ‚úì

üé® Trick #6: Vowel-Consonant Pattern

What it does: Identifies vowel/consonant based patterns

Vowels to remember: A, E, I, O, U (positions: 1, 5, 9, 15, 21)

Question: Find the next: A, B, E, F, I, ?
Pattern: Vowel ‚Üí Next consonant ‚Üí Vowel ‚Üí Next consonant
Answer: J ‚úì

‚è© Trick #7: Skip Pattern Shortcut

What it does: Quickly finds nth letter in skip pattern

Formula: If skipping by n, position = start position + (term √ó n)

Question: In series A, D, G, J..., what is the 10th term?
Pattern: +3 skip
Solution: Position = 1 + (9 √ó 3) = 1 + 27 = 28
But 28 > 26, so 28 - 26 = 2
Answer: B ‚úì

üî¢ Trick #8: Position Sum/Difference

What it does: Solves questions asking for position relationships

Question: Sum of positions of C, A, T?
Solution: C=3, A=1, T=20 ‚Üí 3+1+20 = 24
Answer: 24 ‚úì
Question: Difference between S and F?
Solution: S=19, F=6 ‚Üí 19-6 = 13
Answer: 13 ‚úì

üé≠ Trick #9: Reverse Alphabet Trick

What it does: Handles reverse alphabet questions

Reverse alphabet: Z(1), Y(2), X(3)... A(26)

Quick conversion: Reverse position = 27 - Normal position

Question: What is the 5th letter from the end?
Solution: 5th from end = 27 - 5 = 22 from start = V
Answer: V ‚úì

‚ö° Trick #10: Missing Letter in Series

What it does: Finds missing letters in complex series

Method: Write positions below letters, find pattern in numbers

Question: B, E, I, N, ?
Write positions: 2, 5, 9, 14, ?
Find pattern: +3, +4, +5, +6
Next position: 14 + 6 = 20 = T
Answer: T ‚úì
